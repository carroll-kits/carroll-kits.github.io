Zipline Racer
Instructions

Follow along to build your own Zipline Racer.

Materials

  • 1 Propeller Blade
  • 1 Propeller Stick
  • 1 Propeller End Hook
  • 1 Large Rubberband
  • 8 Popsicle Sticks
  • 1 Straw
  • 1 Bundle of String
  • Glue (not provided)
  • Scissors (not provided)
  • Tape (optional)

Step 1: Gather your materials and lay them out in your workspace. We recommend using cardboard or some other material to protect the table from getting dirty.

Step 2: Connect the propeller blade and the end hook to the propeller stick as shown below. Make sure the two hooks are on the same side of the stick, or you will not be able to connect the rubberband. We recommend using glue to keep the pieces attached. Wait up to 30 minutes for the glue to dry before proceeding.

Step 3: Tie the two ends of the rubber band in a knot so it forms a loop. Slide the knotted end of the rubber band into the end hook as shown below. Loop the rubber band through the propeller hooks 3 times (starting with the end hook, pull it through the propeller hook, back to the end hook, then finish at the propeller hook).

TEST: Test your propeller by winding the blade clockwise several times to tighten the rubberband, then release it into the air.

Step 4: Remove the rubberband to give yourself more space to build. Put a thin line of glue on 2 popsicle sticks and attach them to the propeller stick as shown. Hold them tightly for 1 minute so the glue can set.

Step 5: Cut the straw to the length of a posicle stick. You may either glue or tape the straw between 2 popsicle sticks, but we recommend using tape if you have some. If you do use glue, use a generous amount and hold the sticks in place for at least a minute so the glue can set.

Step 6: Glue 2 popsicle sticks vertically to the sticks you glued to the sides during Step 4. Position them at the end of the sticks closest to the propeller.
IMPORTANT: Be sure the sticks point in the opposite direction of the hooks. In the picture, see that our hooks are pointing down, but the sticks are pointing up. Hold the sticks tightly for 1 minute so the glue can set.

Step 7: Glue 2 more popsicle sticks vertically to the other end of the sticks you glued to the sides during Step 4. Hold them tightly for a minute so the glue can set. Be sure your racer matches the image below, then wait 30 minutes so the glue can fully dry.

Step 8: Glue the two sticks holding the straw inside the top of the sticks you glued vertically. Make sure you use glue at all 4 points of contact. Hold the sticks tightly for 1 minute so the glue can set, then wait 30 minutes so the glue can fully dry.

Step 9: Take your bundle of string and tie one end to a chair or door handle. Unwind the string fully, and feed the other end through the straw so that the propeller is facing towards the first end you tied. Secure the second end of the string by tying it to another chair (or have a helper hold it for you). Make sure the string is pulled tight so that it does not droop. Wind the propeller several times to tighten the rubber band, then release your Zipline Racer!
